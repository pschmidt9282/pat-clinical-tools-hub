Chronic Rhinosinusitis & Allergic Rhinitis Step-Up Algorithm

Symptom classification, intranasal spray technique, tiered pharmacotherapy step-up, and ENT referral indicators.

AAO-HNS / ARIA Guidelines

1. Clinical Symptom Classifier

Select predominant symptoms and clinical history:

AERD / Samter's Triad Detected!
Asthma + Nasal Polyps + Aspirin Intolerance. Indicates severe eosinophilic airway disease. Highly responsive to Leukotriene Receptor Antagonists (Montelukast) and ENT evaluation for biologics (Dupilumab).

2. Tailored Step-Up Regimen

Proper Intranasal Corticosteroid Spray Technique ("Nose to Toes")

Poor spray technique is the #1 cause of treatment failure and epistaxis (nosebleeds) in primary care.

1 Clear Passages
Blow nose gently. Use saline sinus rinse 10-15 mins prior to clear crusts/mucus.
2 Nose to Toes Position
Tilt head slightly forward looking at toes (do NOT tilt head back!).
3 Aim Away From Septum
Use right hand for left nostril, left hand for right nostril. Aim nozzle laterally toward outer corner of eye/ear.
4 Gentle Sniff
Depress pump while gently sniffing in. Do NOT snort hard (prevents medicine swallowing).
