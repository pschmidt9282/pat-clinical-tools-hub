CKD Nephroprotective Therapy Sequencing

Interactive guide for initiating and layering the four pillars of nephroprotection (RASi + SGLT2i + Finerenone + GLP-1 RA). Structure clinical sequencing based on glomerular filtration rate (eGFR), albuminuria (UACR), and serum potassium levels.

KDIGO 2024/2026 Treatment Sequencing: Begin with maximum tolerated **RASi** (ACEi/ARB) for any patient with albuminuria (UACR ≥30 mg/g). Add **SGLT2 inhibitor** down to eGFR of 20 mL/min. Introduce **Finerenone** (if T2D and K⁺ ≤4.8) or **GLP-1 RA** (if T2D) to complete cardiorenal protection.

Pillar 1: RAS Inhibitors ACEi (Lisinopril, etc.) or ARB (Losartan, etc.) Universal (Mandatory in Albuminuria) Any eGFR (Start with caution if eGFR <30) ≥ 30 mg/g (Strongest if ≥300) Raises Potassium (Hyperkalemia risk) Pillar 1 (Foundational) Slows eGFR decline, reduces progression to ESKD, lowers CV mortality. Creatinine and K⁺ at 1–2 weeks. Allow up to 30% creatinine rise.
Pillar 2: SGLT2 Inhibitors Dapagliflozin (Farxiga), Empagliflozin (Jardiance) Recommended (All CKD down to eGFR 20) eGFR 20 (Can continue below 20 until dialysis) ≥ 200 mg/g (FDA); ≥ 30 mg/g (KDIGO) Lowers Potassium (Buffers RASi hyperkalemia) Pillar 2 (Initiate immediately after RASi) Reduces ESKD by ~35%, lowers CV death and heart failure hospitalizations. Check volume/BP. Hold during acute sickness/fasting/surgery (DKA risk).
Pillar 3: Finerenone Kerendia (Non-Steroidal MRA) Type 2 Diabetes + CKD only eGFR 25 (Do not initiate if eGFR <25) ≥ 30 mg/g (with retinopathy) or ≥ 300 mg/g Raises Potassium (Less than spironolactone) Pillar 3 (Add after RASi + SGLT2i are optimized) Reduces composite renal events (FIDELIO-DKD) and CV events (FIGARO-DKD). **Baseline K⁺ must be ≤4.8 mEq/L to start**. Monitor K⁺ at 4 weeks, hold if >5.5.
Pillar 4: GLP-1 RAs Semaglutide (Ozempic) Type 2 Diabetes + CKD only eGFR 15 (Safe down to dialysis) ≥ 30 mg/g Potassium Neutral Pillar 4 (Use for residual glycemic/weight/renal risk) Reduces kidney failure composite and CV death (FLOW trial), significant weight loss. GI tolerability. Titrate slowly at 4-week intervals.
