Antihypertensive Agents — First-Line Selection

Compare first-line antihypertensive classes (Thiazide Diuretics, CCBs, ACEi, and ARBs) alongside high-yield within-class agent selections. Select choices using CKD, Heart Failure, stroke history, demographics, and clinical trials (ALLHAT, Diuretic Comparison Project).

Race & Demographics: Initial therapy in Black adults without HF or CKD should start with a Thiazide-type diuretic or CCB. If an ACEi/ARB is required for albuminuria in Black patients, ARBs are preferred over ACEi due to a 3-fold higher risk of angioedema.
