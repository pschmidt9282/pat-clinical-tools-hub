CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING (SKIN ATROPHY): Chronic, unsupervised use of high-potency topical corticosteroids (TCS) leads to skin thinning (atrophy), striae, telangiectasias, and systemic HPA axis suppression. Restrict Class 1 steroids (e.g. Clobetasol) to <2 consecutive weeks, and avoid high potency altogether on the face, eyelids, and skin folds.

Key Prescribing Considerations

  • Urea Concentrations: Urea ≤10% acts as a humectant/moisturizer. Urea >10% adds mild keratolytic properties. Urea ≥40% is strongly keratolytic and reserved for severe hyperkeratotic plaques (e.g., heels, palms) and is Rx-only.
  • AmLactin vs. Rx Ammonium Lactate: AmLactin (12% lactic acid) is the OTC equivalent of prescription ammonium lactate 12%. The active ingredient is identical, but the OTC version is marketed as a cosmetic moisturizer.
  • Stepwise Triage: For moderate xerosis, a urea 10% cream or AmLactin BID is a reasonable first step. For severe scaling/hyperkeratosis, prescribe ammonium lactate 12% or urea 20–40%.
  • Combination Synergy: Products combining humectants (e.g., urea, glycerol) with occlusives (petrolatum) or barrier lipids (ceramides) offer additive clinical benefits.

Application & Safety Counseling

  • Timing: Emollients should be applied to clean, slightly damp skin when possible (e.g., immediately after bathing) to trap moisture.
  • Broken Skin Warning: Lactic acid and urea-containing products may cause transient stinging/burning on broken, fissured, or inflamed skin. Avoid applying to active open wounds.
  • Sun Safety: Alpha-hydroxy acids (lactic acid/ammonium lactate) increase sun sensitivity. Counsel patients to minimize sun exposure and apply sunscreen on treated areas.
