Potassium Binders for Hyperkalemia

Interactive comparative matrix of oral potassium-binding agents (SPS, Patiromer, Sodium Zirconium Cyclosilicate). Evaluate binders by onset speed, GI necrosis warning status, RAASi-enablement evidence (AMBER trial), and cost.

FDA Safety Boxed Warnings: SPS carries a warning for intestinal necrosis (linked to bowel ischemia and perforation). Patiromer and SZC do not carry this warning. Edema is a specific concern with SZC (Lokelma) due to its high sodium content (~400 mg sodium per 10g dose).
