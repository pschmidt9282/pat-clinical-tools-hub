Guidelines mandate a triple-combination regimen including a CCB, an ACEi or ARB, and a Thiazide/Thiazide-like Diuretic at maximum tolerated doses.

Clinical Tip: Switch Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) to Chlorthalidone 25mg or Indapamide 2.5mg. Chlorthalidone has a 1.5–2x longer half-life (45-60 hrs) and significantly greater 24-hr nocturnal BP reduction compared to HCTZ.

4th-Line Recommendation: Spironolactone (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist)
• Initial Dose: Spironolactone 12.5 mg to 25 mg PO daily.
• Titration: May titrate up to 50 mg daily if needed.
• Monitoring: Re-check Serum Potassium and Creatinine at 1 week and 4 weeks after initiation.
• Gynecomastia Note: If painful mastodynia/gynecomastia develops, switch to Eplerenone 50 mg daily.
