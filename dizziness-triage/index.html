Outpatient Dizziness Triage & Management

Differentiate BPPV vs. Peripheral Neuritis vs. Central Stroke using HINTS and targeted maneuvers.

AAN / HINTS Standard

1. Clinical Presentation Triage

HINTS Exam Module

Only for Continuous Vertigo (AVS)

Validity Warning: HINTS is 99% sensitive for stroke ONLY when performed on patients with ongoing, continuous vertigo and nystagmus at rest.

2. Triage Output & Management Protocol

Posterior Canal BPPV: Step-by-Step Epley Maneuver

Perform following positive Dix-Hallpike test (upbeating & torsional nystagmus towards affected down-ear):

Step 1 Affected Ear Down
Turn head 45° toward affected ear. Rapidly lay patient back with neck extended 20° off edge of table (Dix-Hallpike position). Hold for 30–60 seconds post-nystagmus.
Step 2 Rotate Head 90° Opposite
Without raising head, slowly rotate head 90° toward the healthy ear (facing 45° away). Hold for 30–60 seconds.
Step 3 Roll onto Side Facing Floor
Patient rolls onto shoulder of healthy side. Head rotates another 90° looking down at floor (nose at 45° downward). Hold for 30–60 seconds.
Step 4 Sit Upright & Re-center
Slowly bring patient up to sitting position while maintaining head turn. Once seated, return head to midline with slight forward chin tilt.
